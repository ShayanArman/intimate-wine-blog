## Where in Tuscany should wine lovers start?

> Let's start with this: we live here.

> Right in the heart of Chianti. In Greve in Chianti, to be exact, the center of Chianti Classico (it's in the name), where the cobblestones are worn smooth by generations of footsteps and the pace of life is still set by the sun, the seasons, and the harvest.

> Chianti is a region where time slows. It's where mornings are for walks among olive groves, afternoons for long lunches under wisteria, and evenings are best spent with a bottle uncorked and a story or two.

## Is it better to follow a wine route every day or stay in a villa and taste there?

> When dreaming of an idyllic getaway, few places evoke the sense of charm and tranquility like a villa in Tuscany.

> One of the primary benefits of hosting a wine tasting at your villa is the sheer convenience and relaxation it offers. There's no need to travel from vineyard to vineyard. Instead, you can sit back and savour the wines while taking in the breathtaking views from your villa's terrace or garden.

> Tuscany's winding roads are beautiful but can be challenging, especially after a few glasses of wine. An in-villa tasting eliminates the worry about driving, allowing you to fully enjoy the experience without any safety concerns.
