Email was invented in 1971. More than fifty years later, most of us still manage our inboxes the same way — one message at a time. That's about to change.

## The Problem at Scale

The average professional receives **121 emails per day**. Over a year, that's nearly 44,000 messages competing for attention. Traditional filters and folders were designed for a world of dozens of emails, not thousands. They've buckled under the weight.

The result is "inbox anxiety" — the low-grade stress that comes from knowing there's always something unread, unsorted, or un-dealt-with lurking in your inbox.

## How Zero Inbox Uses AI

Zero Inbox applies large-language-model intelligence directly to your mailbox:

- **Semantic categorization** — Instead of relying on keyword rules, our models read and understand the *intent* of every email, sorting them into meaningful groups in seconds.
- **Bulk actions with one click** — Once emails are categorized, you can delete, archive, or label thousands of messages at once.
- **Adaptive learning** — The system watches which emails you keep and which you discard, refining its understanding of what matters *to you* over time.

## What's Next

We're currently building **Smart Summaries** — a feature that distills long email threads into concise, actionable briefs so you never have to scroll through a 47-reply chain again.

AI isn't replacing email. It's making email work the way it always should have.
